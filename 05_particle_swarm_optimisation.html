Particle Swarm Optimisation

Position and velocity updates, pbest and gbest, and the parameters that govern continuous optimisation in PSO.

Practical View

PSO is a population-based metaheuristic for continuous optimisation. Each particle stores a position, a velocity, its own best known position, and the best known position of the swarm.

Search space: continuous
Memory: pbest and gbest
Main equations: velocity and position

Algorithm

1
Initialise positions, velocities, and personal bests.

Each particle starts with some x_i, v_i, and initial pbest_i.

2
Evaluate fitness.

For minimisation, lower fitness values are better.

3
Update personal bests.

If the current position beats a particle's old best, store the new one.

4
Update the global best.

If any particle beats the swarm best, store it as gbest.

5
Update velocity and then position.

Use the two standard equations.

6
Repeat until termination.

Stop after a maximum number of iterations or once improvement stalls.

Particle Components

ComponentSymbolMeaning
Positionx_iThe current candidate solution
Velocityv_iThe step size and direction for movement
Personal bestpbest_iBest position this particle has found so far
Global bestgbestBest position found by the swarm

Velocity Update Equation

v_i(t+1) = w * v_i(t) + l1 * r1 * (pbest_i - x_i) + l2 * r2 * (gbest - x_i)
TermRole
w * v_i(t)Inertia term that keeps some momentum from the previous move
l1 * r1 * (pbest_i - x_i)Cognitive term that pulls the particle toward its own best solution
l2 * r2 * (gbest - x_i)Social term that pulls the particle toward the swarm's best solution

Position Update Equation

x_i(t+1) = x_i(t) + v_i(t+1)

The new position is just the old position plus the new velocity. In practice, exam questions often ask you to compute one full iteration using the velocity equation first and then the position equation second.

Worked Calculation Pattern

Even when the exact numbers change, your answer structure should stay the same:

1
Write current values.

List x_i(t), v_i(t), pbest_i, gbest, w, l1, l2, r1, and r2.

2
Compute the inertia term.

Multiply the current velocity by w.

3
Compute the cognitive term.

Calculate how far the particle is from pbest_i.

4
Compute the social term.

Calculate how far the particle is from gbest.

5
Add the three pieces to get v_i(t+1).

Then plug that value into the position update equation.

Parameters You Should Be Ready To Explain

ParameterRole
Swarm sizeNumber of particles exploring the space
VmaxMaximum velocity limit to stop particles overshooting too aggressively
Inertia weight wBalances momentum against change of direction
Cognitive factor l1Strength of attraction to personal best
Social factor l2Strength of attraction to global best
Random values r1, r2Add stochasticity to the search
Termination criterionStops the run after enough iterations or insufficient improvement
The exploration versus exploitation balance comes mainly from the interaction of w, l1, and l2.

Full Numerical Demonstration

Use the following values for one particle in a 1D minimisation problem:

ValueSetting
x_t10
v_t2
pbest5
gbest12
w0.8
l12.0
l22.0
r1, r20.5 and 0.3
v(t+1) = w*v(t) + l1*r1*(pbest - x) + l2*r2*(gbest - x) = 0.8*2 + 2.0*0.5*(5 - 10) + 2.0*0.3*(12 - 10) = 1.6 + 1.0*(-5) + 0.6*(2) = 1.6 - 5.0 + 1.2 = -2.2 x(t+1) = x(t) + v(t+1) = 10 + (-2.2) = 7.8

The negative new velocity means the particle moves left from 10 to 7.8.

How To Interpret The Numbers

Why did the particle move left? Its personal best at 5 is much better than its current position 10, so the cognitive pull is strongly negative.
Why did it not jump all the way to 5 or 12? PSO does not teleport. It updates velocity first, then moves by that velocity, so the motion is gradual and history-sensitive.
